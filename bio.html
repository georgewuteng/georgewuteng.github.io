George Wu Teng

  1. bio

    2. George Wu Teng was born in 1995 in Manassas, Virginia.

    He attended the Walnut Hill School for the Arts in Natick, Massachusetts after he was expelled from two other high schools.

    He went on to attend McGill University, where he completed his Bachelor of Music in piano performance, with dual minors in English literature and psychology.


    He has worked as:

    • an uncommitted web developer
    • quite a skilled dishwasher
    • an enthusiastic line cook
    • a slightly less enthusiastic prep cook
    • an entirely unenthusiastic server
    • a bored stagehand
    • an almost-catatonic stage manager
    • an amphetamine-fueled editor/proofreader
    • a very happy piano teacher
    • an ice cream scooper



    He is currently pursuing his master's degree at the Université de Montréal under the mentorship of Paul Stewart. George's writing has been published both online and in-print.

    George's principal teachers include Kyoko Hashimoto, chair of piano at McGill University, Angel Ramón Rivera of the New England Conservatory, Paul Orgel of the University of Vermont, and Yoshie Akimoto. He has also studied with Elinor Freer of the Eastman School of Music, and Douglas Humpherys, Eastman's chair of piano. He is consistently astounded and grateful that anyone accepts him into their studio, or agrees to work with him.


    In his free time, George eats arugula-based salads and takes extremely long walks. He is often sweating.
