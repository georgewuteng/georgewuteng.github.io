    2. 14 June 2021

    Finished practicing at the faculty today, and, you know, was once again reminded of how much better my overall mental hygiene is when I practice on a "real" grand piano, as opposed to a digital one, or even an upright. Not really sure why I somehow manage to forget this simple fact with such frequency; regardless, it'll be nice next semester when things are more or less returning to normalcy, and almost all of my practicing will be done in these practice rooms.

    Deafheaven released a new single recently, and I somehow hadn't listened to it prior to today. I'm a little torn on the clean vocals, and an overall blandness, but I still have high hopes for this next album as their songwriting is still the best in any black metal band making music these days (YOU CAN QUOTE ME).

    Also extremely disappointed Tsitsipas couldn't upend Djokovic at Roland Garros; really stunning match regardless. Another also: where is Kyrgios? (I know of his reasons for not participating in many tournaments during "these times," and also find it hilarious that another reason he gave for sitting out the French Open was that he just "doesn't like clay.") Watching Djokovic v. Tsitsipas may finally get me to watch Borg v. McEnroe, that 2017 movie. Something about more-or-less-cookie-cutter sports dramas always get to me.

    To See the Next Part of the Dream, by Parannoul, is finally on Spotify. Go listen to it. And here's a poem I was reminded of when sending some poems I really like to a friend of mine:
